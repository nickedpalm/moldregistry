## How to Choose a Mold Inspector in Fort Worth

**Look for real credentials.** CMI (Certified Mold Inspector) and ACAC CMC (Council-certified Microbial Consultant) are the gold standard. These aren't participation trophies — they require documented training, exam passage, and continuing education. A Texas-licensed home inspector who dabbles in mold is not the same as someone who specializes in it. Ask directly: "What certifications do you hold?" If they hem and haw, move on.

**Verify they do more than visual inspections.** A legitimate inspector will do moisture mapping (thermal imaging or moisture meters to find hidden dampness), air sampling, and surface sampling if needed. Samples go to a third-party lab — not their own in-house lab. That distance matters for credibility. Visual-only inspections miss problems behind walls.

**Ask about their remediation stance.** This is the sour spot in the industry: some inspectors have financial ties to remediation companies. It's a conflict of interest baked into their recommendation. A clean shop will inspect independently and refer you to remediation firms without kickback arrangements. Ask: "Are you affiliated with any remediation contractors?" Straight answer or red flag.

**Check turnaround on reports.** You should have a detailed written report within 3-5 business days, including species identification, moisture source analysis, affected areas, and remediation recommendations. If they hand you a handwritten summary and say "we'll email the full thing later," that's not a professional outfit.

**Pro Tip:** Fort Worth's humid subtropical climate (hot, wet summers) creates perfect conditions for mold. Inspectors familiar with local building codes and regional moisture patterns will ask better questions about your HVAC maintenance, attic ventilation, and foundation drainage — not just "do you see mold?"

## What to Expect

Inspections run $200–$800 depending on the home's size, the scope (visual-only vs. sampling), and whether moisture mapping is included. Most inspectors charge a flat rate for a standard residential inspection; sampling adds cost per sample ($50–$150 each, sent to the lab). Turnaround on the full report is typically 3–7 business days once lab results come back.

The process itself takes 2–4 hours. The inspector will document moisture issues, check HVAC systems, examine crawl spaces and attics, and take photos. If sampling happens, you'll get a lab report showing mold species, spore counts, and indoor vs. outdoor comparisons. A good report tells you not just *what* is there, but *why* — is it active moisture intrusion, poor ventilation, a past leak that dried out, condensation on AC lines?

**Reality Check:** Cheap inspectors often low-ball pricing to land the job, then upsell sampling or push you toward their remediation partner. Budget for the real cost upfront. A $300 inspection is usually thorough. A $150 inspection usually isn't.
