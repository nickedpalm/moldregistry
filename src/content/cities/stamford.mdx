## How to Choose a Mold Inspector in Stamford

**Look for a CMI or ACAC credential first.** A Certified Mold Inspector (CMI) or ACAC-certified professional (CMC, CMRS, or CIEC) has completed formal training, passed an exam, and carries continuing education requirements. Connecticut doesn't mandate mold inspector licensing, so credentials are your clearest signal of competence. If someone's business card just says "mold inspector" with no letters after their name, ask why.

**Verify they do on-site moisture mapping and lab testing.** A visual inspection alone isn't enough—you need someone who brings moisture meters, thermal imaging, and the ability to collect air and surface samples sent to an independent third-party lab. If they're not doing this, they're guessing. Ask upfront: "Will you provide moisture mapping and lab results in your report?"

**Ask about their turnaround on lab results.** Reputable inspectors will tell you they send samples to a certified lab and results typically arrive within 7–10 business days. Anyone promising results faster than that should raise a red flag—legitimate testing takes time.

**Check that they're not also the remediation company.** This is the classic conflict of interest. If the same person who inspects for mold also sells you a $15,000 remediation contract, you've already lost. Find an inspector who reports findings without a financial stake in the solution.

**Pro Tip:** Stamford's proximity to Long Island Sound and Connecticut's humid climate means water intrusion and condensation issues are common, especially in older homes and buildings built before modern moisture barriers were standard. A local inspector will know the typical problem zones—basement walls, crawl spaces, HVAC ducts—and won't waste time on unlikely areas.

## What to Expect

A typical mold inspection in Stamford runs $300–600 for a single-family home, though larger properties or multi-unit buildings can go higher. The inspector will spend 2–4 hours on-site doing a visual assessment, moisture readings, and collecting samples if warranted. You'll get a detailed report within 1–2 weeks that identifies any mold species found, the extent of growth, the likely source of moisture, and a remediation protocol if needed.

**Reality Check:** Avoid anyone who quotes you a price and then tries to upsell you remediation work on the spot. That's not how this works. The inspection is separate from the fix. Get the report first, understand the scope, then get competitive quotes from licensed remediation contractors if you need them.
