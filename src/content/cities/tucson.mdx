## How to Choose a Mold Inspector in Tucson

**Look for the right credentials first.** A CMI (Certified Mold Inspector) has passed a standardized exam and understands moisture dynamics, building science, and mold ecology. If they're also an ACAC CMC (Council-certified Microbial Consultant), they can interpret lab results and recommend remediation protocols. Don't settle for "certified by online course X"—verify credentials on the issuing body's website. In Arizona, there's no state licensure requirement for mold inspectors, which means credentials are your actual safety net.

**Verify they do moisture mapping, not just visual inspection.** A competent inspector brings a moisture meter and thermal imaging camera. They're not guessing where water intrusion happened—they're finding it. This matters because mold grows where moisture is, not where you think it is. If someone quotes you without mentioning moisture assessment, keep looking.

**Ask if they send samples to a third-party lab.** If they own the lab or "read samples in-house," there's a conflict of interest. You want results from an independent AIHA-accredited lab so the data is defensible in court or with your insurance company. This adds 3–5 days to turnaround, but it's worth it.

**Pro Tip:** Tucson's low humidity and intense sun mean roof leaks and monsoon water intrusion are the main culprits, not ambient moisture. Ask your inspector if they've handled post-monsoon assessments—experience with local seasonal patterns matters.

## What to Expect

A standard mold inspection runs $300–$600 for a single-family home, depending on square footage and complexity. That fee covers a 2–3 hour visual assessment, moisture mapping, and air/surface sampling if needed. Lab results typically arrive within a week. If remediation is recommended, that's a separate project with its own cost structure (handled by a remediation contractor, ideally one certified as an ACAC CMRS).

**Reality Check:** Avoid flat-rate "mold inspections" under $200—that's usually just a walk-through with no equipment or samples. You're paying for expertise and data, not a visual opinion you could get from a handyman.
