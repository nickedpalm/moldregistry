## How to Choose a Mold Inspector in Des Moines

- **Verify credentials first.** Look for CMI (Certified Mold Inspector), ACAC CMC (Council-certified Microbial Consultant), or ACAC CMRS designations. These require continuing education and adherence to standards. A business card with a "certified" claim but no acronym or issuing body? Skip it.

- **Ask if they handle moisture mapping.** Visual inspection alone misses problems. You want someone who brings thermal imaging and moisture meters to find water intrusion before it becomes visible mold. This is standard practice; if they're not offering it, they're cutting corners.

- **Confirm lab relationships.** Your inspector should send samples to a third-party lab, not their own backyard analysis. Ask which lab they use and why. Reputable inspectors are transparent about this—if they get defensive, that's a red flag.

- **Check turnaround on reports.** Good inspectors deliver findings within 5-7 business days. Faster isn't always better (they might be skipping steps), but anything longer than two weeks suggests they're juggling too many jobs or waiting on backlogged testing.

- **Pro Tip:** Call two inspectors before booking. One question to both: "If you find mold, what species is it likely to be, and how do you determine the source?" If they answer in generalities, move on. The inspector you want can explain the connection between moisture source and colonization pattern.

## What to Expect

A typical residential mold inspection in Des Moines runs $250–$600, depending on home size, accessibility, and whether sampling is included (visual-only inspections sit at the lower end; samples sent to labs cost more). You're paying for expertise, not just a walkthrough. The process usually takes 2–4 hours. The inspector documents the findings, compiles images and lab results, and delivers a detailed report outlining what they found, the likely cause, and remediation recommendations. Most inspectors turn around reports within a week.

**Reality Check:** If someone quotes you $150 for a full inspection including lab work, they're either selling you something else or cutting a corner you'll pay for later. Legitimate testing costs money. Budget accordingly.
